Veterans Memorial Bridge Construction.

Albert Stone is noted for having a special photographic interest in the grand construction projects that were happening in Rochester at the time he was working. One of the most significant of these projects was the construction of the Veterans Memorial Bridge completed in December, 1931. The bridge spans the Genesee river about one mile North of lower falls with a length of 981 feet. One newspaper reported “Its attractiveness is increase [sic] by the natural scenic beauty of the lower river, the span being in one of the most picturesque sections of the Genesee gorge.” (1) The bridge was constructed out of granite-faced concrete and has a total of seven arches spanning the Genesee.

At the dedication of the bridge, the president of World War I Gold Star Mothers said following:

Great bridges across flowing waters are

indicative of the continual labors of life.

In the very presence of continual change,

they seem to connect the past with the

present, even as they connect the East

with the West, the North with the South...

(2)
