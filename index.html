Albert R. Stone Negative Collection

A Spatial-Historical Perspective

The goal of this website is to interactively display the Albert R. Stone Negative Collection. Albert R. Stone was a photographer for the Democrat and Chronicle and the Rochester Herald, two newspapers published in Rochester, New York. Stone worked from 1904 to 1936, a period of serious transformation and growth in the city. The Albert R. Stone Negative Collection consists of 14,000 glass negatives and is held at the Rochester Museum and Science Center (RMSC). The collection covers a range of subjects in the region, but holds a particular focus on the city’s transportation infrastructure. It is a hugely important collection for early 20th century urban history and the heritage of the City of Rochester.

Photo scans of the negatives are available through the RMSC’s online catalogue. The goal of this project is to lift the collection from those archives and surface them in a spatially informed way. Many of the images contain visual cues about their original locations, and the associated notes for the images sometimes tell us the location of their creation specifically. The goal is that this project will open up new avenues for exploration of this important collection. To get started, navigate to the map and explore images there. The "Case Studies" section gives examles of how the project can provide insight for historical inquiry.
